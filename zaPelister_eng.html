Pelister National Park



We do not always give importance to the things that nature give us. My homeland, Macedonia, is full of beautiful places, such as the Pelister National Park.

This national park is located on Baba Mountain, near the city, Bitola. It was declared a national park in 1948, and is the first national park in Macedonia.




Flora

The flora in this park is unusual and diverse. It can be said that despite all the types of plants, the main fauna is still the pine Molika. It is one of the reasons for declaring Pelister a national park.



The Molika pine was discovered by the botanist Henrich August Rudolf Grisebach in 1839, and was announced as a new species for science in 1844. There are pines on Pelister that are older than 230 years. Molika is a five-needle pine, and is the most common, widespread and most important plant form in the Pelister National Park. It is a Balkan endemic and a trademark of Pelister National Park.



Фауна

Normally, this park does not lack wildlife. It has a rich and diverse wildlife that can be mentioned: the balkan lynx, the balkan chamois, diurnal butterflies, various species of birds and etc.




Хидрологија

Pelister is rich in water springs, streams and mountain rivers, and there are two glacial lakes. From the rivers, I would mention the river Semnica, which is the longest watercourse on Pelister with a length of 46 km. The glacial lakes are the Big and the Small Lake which are also called “Pelister Eyes”. They are about 2 km away from each other. They also belong to the group of permanent lakes. Specific for the Big Lake is that it is one of the highest glacial lakes in Macedonia. In the Pelister lakes live the endemic Pelister stream trout and the Pelagonian stream trout.




Зошто Пелистер?

Pelister National Park is a place that with its natural beauties can take your breath away. Pelister offers clean air at a time when it is rare to find it, recreation whether, a walk along the various paths through which you can explore the beautiful flora and fauna in spring and summer, or skiing on the slopes of Baba Mountain in winter.



But in my opinion it must be noted that all these beauties that this national park offers us, we do not protect and value enough, we as citizens of Macedonia, but also Macedonia as a country. I think this is not only a case for this park, but also for the national treasures we have in general. But this is not only happening in our country, of course, this is happening globally. I personally consider it a big problem and I think that the environmental awareness of people should be awakened as soon as possible. Examples of this are deforestation, pollution of nature by dumping, for example. Yes, this may not happen in national parks, but I still think it is important to understand that even if it is not declared a national treasure, it should not be destroyed. I hope that with this paragraph I will encourage more people to think about it and try to participate in finding a solution to the problem.
